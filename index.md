Niagara Assembly Center (NAC) is a vertically integrated electronics manufacturing and technology company headquartered in Hamilton, Ontario. We are establishing the first purpose-built facility in North America to combine small-batch PCB assembly, firmware-level optimization, workforce education, and ecosystem coordination under a single roof — and a single strategic vision. The result is a new kind of manufacturing marketplace.

Our model is inspired by the extraordinary clustering effect witnessed in the Essex County greenhouse corridor — a geography once overlooked that transformed itself into a world-class agricultural production hub through density, specialization, and inter-firm coordination. NAC intends to do for advanced electronics manufacturing in the Niagara Peninsula what Kingsville did for protected-crop agriculture: build a resilient, export-ready, innovation-dense industrial ecosystem from the ground up.

**The timing is ideal.** North American governments and corporations are actively reshoring electronics manufacturing. The Niagara Peninsula — spanning Hamilton, Brantford, Port Dover, and Fort Erie on the Canadian side, with direct cross-border access to Buffalo and Western New York — offers underutilized industrial capacity, strong post-secondary institutions, and significant regional incentives. NAC is positioned to be the anchor institution that activates this potential.
