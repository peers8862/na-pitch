The Production & Assembly Lead is the technical cornerstone of NAC's manufacturing floor. This individual is responsible for the end-to-end execution of printed circuit board assembly operations, encompassing surface-mount technology (SMT), through-hole technology (THT), inspection, rework, and quality certification. As a founding team member, this role carries both hands-on production responsibility and a strategic mandate to establish processes, document standards, and build scalable workflows that can support team growth and client acquisition across the Niagara–Buffalo corridor.

## Core Responsibilities

- Lead all SMT and THT assembly operations including solder paste application, component placement, reflow profiling, wave soldering, and hand soldering for small-batch and prototype production runs.
- Establish and enforce IPC-A-610 workmanship standards across all production outputs; serve as primary IPC-certified evaluator and point of contact for quality audits.
- Develop and maintain production travellers, assembly documentation, bill-of-materials (BOM) verification procedures, and first-article inspection (FAI) protocols.
- Operate and maintain key production equipment including SMT pick-and-place systems, reflow ovens, wave solder machines, AOI systems, and rework stations; coordinate preventive maintenance schedules.
- Manage raw material intake, component inventory, storage conditions (ESD, moisture sensitivity), and outbound shipment preparation in coordination with the Operations Manager.
- Interface directly with clients during design-for-manufacture (DFM) reviews to identify assembly risk, provide panelization guidance, and translate design files into production-ready formats.
- Collaborate with the Firmware & Embedded Systems Engineer to support board bring-up, in-circuit testing (ICT), and boundary scan procedures during initial production cycles.
- Contribute to the Workforce Education pillar by mentoring trainees, supporting lab development, and delivering technical instruction as required.

## Required Qualifications

- Minimum 5 years of hands-on PCB assembly experience in a production environment, with demonstrated competency across SMT and THT processes.
- IPC-A-610 CIS (Certified IPC Specialist) certification required; CIT (Certified IPC Trainer) designation is a strong asset.
- Proficiency with industry-standard ERP/MRP systems and production documentation practices.
- Demonstrated experience with DFM/DFA analysis and the ability to read and interpret Gerber files, assembly drawings, and schematics.
- Working knowledge of ESD control, moisture sensitivity levels (MSL), and cleanroom-adjacent handling procedures.
- Experience with statistical process control (SPC), defect root cause analysis, and corrective action processes.

## Preferred Qualifications

- IPC-7711/7721 rework and repair certification.
- Experience in a mixed-technology, low-volume high-mix (LVHM) production environment.
- Familiarity with ITAR-controlled or regulated electronics manufacturing environments.
- Prior experience establishing a new production line or launching a contract manufacturing operation.

## Competencies

- **Precision and attention to detail** under production-paced conditions.
- **Clear technical communication** with both engineering and non-engineering stakeholders.
- **Process ownership mindset:** the ability to build systems, not just follow them.
- **Collaborative approach** to cross-functional work, particularly with firmware and operations teams.
