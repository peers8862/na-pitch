The Workforce Development & Training Coordinator leads NAC's third pillar: building the regional talent pipeline and institutional relationships that transform NAC from an assembly house into an ecosystem anchor. This role designs, delivers, and continuously improves training programs spanning IPC certifications, embedded systems fundamentals, PCB design for manufacturing, and industrial electronics operations. Equally important is the business development dimension of this position: the Coordinator actively cultivates relationships with colleges, apprenticeship programs, municipal workforce agencies, IoT industry associations, and employer partners across the Hamilton–Niagara–Buffalo corridor, positioning NAC as the preferred training and upskilling destination in the region's reindustrialization effort.

## Core Responsibilities

- Design and deliver curriculum for NAC's core training programs: PCB assembly fundamentals, IPC workmanship standards (A-610, J-STD-001), embedded systems introduction, soldering certification, and industry safety.
- Manage the logistics of IPC-certified training delivery including materials procurement, examination administration, and certification record-keeping in accordance with IPC program requirements.
- Develop and maintain relationships with postsecondary partners (Mohawk College, Niagara College, Buffalo-area community colleges) to establish co-op placements, laboratory access agreements, and curriculum articulation pathways.
- Engage municipal workforce development agencies, apprenticeship coordinators, and employment service providers to identify cohort training opportunities and access public workforce funding streams.
- Represent NAC at industry events, IoT association meetings, trade shows, and regional economic development forums; build the company's reputation as a technical training leader.
- Work with the Firmware Engineer and Production Lead to translate technical expertise into instructional content accessible to adult learners at varying skill levels.
- Track program outcomes including certification pass rates, employment placements, employer satisfaction, and return enrollment; use data to refine program design.
- Contribute to grant applications and funding proposals by documenting training program scope, projected participant numbers, and community impact metrics.
- Identify client company upskilling needs and develop custom in-house or on-site training engagements as an additional revenue stream.

## Required Qualifications

- Minimum 4 years of experience in technical training, workforce development, or adult education within a manufacturing, electronics, or engineering context.
- IPC-A-610 CIS certification required; CIT designation or ability to obtain within the first year of employment.
- Demonstrated experience designing technical curriculum for adult learners, including hands-on lab components and competency-based assessment.
- Track record of successfully managing institutional partnerships with colleges, training agencies, or employer groups.
- Familiarity with Canadian workforce funding programs (Canada Job Grant, Apprenticeship Incentive Grant, provincial employment programs) and the ability to support funding applications.
- Strong presentation, facilitation, and interpersonal skills; comfort engaging audiences from shop-floor workers to C-suite stakeholders.

## Preferred Qualifications

- Experience with IoT, electronics, or advanced manufacturing sector workforce initiatives.
- Knowledge of the Ontario college system and pathways to skilled trades certification.
- Cross-border experience or familiarity with New York State workforce development programs.
- Adult education certification (e.g., Ontario College of Teachers, CPDT, or equivalent).
- Background in or exposure to electronics manufacturing or embedded systems technology.

## Competencies

- **Relationship-building:** the ability to earn trust with institutional partners, community organizations, and employer clients.
- **Program ownership:** designing systems that improve over time through measurement and iteration.
- **Mission alignment:** genuine investment in workforce equity and regional economic development.
- **Adaptability:** comfortable moving between classroom facilitation, boardroom presentations, and production floor observation.
