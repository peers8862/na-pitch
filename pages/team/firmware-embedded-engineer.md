This is NAC's most technically differentiated role and the primary driver of the company's core value proposition. The Firmware & Embedded Systems Engineer is responsible for delivering measurable, verifiable performance improvements to client embedded systems through deep-level code optimization — working at the assembly, binary, and register level across target microcontroller architectures. This engineer produces outcomes that standard compiler toolchains cannot: sub-cycle precision in timing-critical routines, aggressive power envelope reduction, and code density improvements that directly extend battery life, reduce BOM cost, and increase device reliability. NAC's competitive differentiation depends on this position delivering demonstrable, benchmarkable results.

## Core Responsibilities

- Perform assembly-level and binary-level optimization of firmware for resource-constrained microcontrollers (AVR, ARM Cortex-M, RISC-V, MSP430, PIC, and related architectures), targeting cycle count reduction, code size minimization, and power consumption improvement.
- Conduct comparative benchmarking between compiler-generated output and hand-optimized implementations; produce quantified performance reports suitable for client delivery and investor/partner demonstrations.
- Develop and maintain NAC's internal library of optimized firmware primitives, interrupt service routines (ISRs), communication protocol handlers (I2C, SPI, UART, CAN), and peripheral initialization routines.
- Perform register-level peripheral configuration, clock tree analysis, and power domain management to achieve measurable reductions in active and sleep-state current draw.
- Support PCB bring-up activities including JTAG/SWD debugging, bootloader development, memory map configuration, and hardware abstraction layer (HAL) implementation or bypass.
- Conduct in-circuit testing and validation using logic analyzers, oscilloscopes, current probes, and protocol analyzers; document results against client-specified acceptance criteria.
- Collaborate with the Production Lead during assembly and test phases to identify hardware-firmware interaction issues and contribute to test fixture design.
- Contribute technical content to the Workforce Education pillar, including lab exercises, instructional materials on embedded architecture, and mentoring of junior staff or students.
- Maintain version-controlled firmware repositories, produce clear technical documentation, and deliver client-facing reports that communicate optimization outcomes in both technical and business terms.

## Required Qualifications

- Degree or equivalent demonstrated experience in Electrical Engineering, Computer Engineering, or a closely related discipline.
- Minimum 4 years of professional embedded firmware development experience with direct exposure to assembly language programming on one or more target architectures.
- Proven ability to read, write, and analyze disassembled machine code; familiarity with linker scripts, map files, and binary analysis tools (objdump, Ghidra, or equivalent).
- Deep working knowledge of at least two MCU architecture families and their associated toolchains, instruction set architectures (ISAs), and peripheral ecosystems.
- Experience with hardware-level debugging tools including JTAG/SWD programmers, logic analyzers, and oscilloscopes.
- Strong understanding of real-time constraints, interrupt latency, stack usage, and memory-mapped I/O.

## Preferred Qualifications

- Demonstrated 60%+ cycle count or code size reduction results from hand-optimization versus compiler baseline (portfolio evidence preferred).
- Experience with ultra-low-power design techniques including dynamic voltage/frequency scaling, tickless RTOS configurations, and aggressive peripheral shutdown sequencing.
- Familiarity with MISRA-C, DO-178C, or IEC 61508 firmware development standards.
- Experience developing or contributing to open-source embedded toolchains, HALs, or RTOS configurations.
- Background in IoT edge device development, industrial sensor nodes, or portable battery-powered instrumentation.

## Competencies

- **Analytical rigor:** the ability to quantify and communicate performance improvements with precision.
- **Systems-level thinking:** understanding how firmware decisions affect hardware cost, power design, and manufacturing.
- **Self-directed problem solving** in low-documentation, novel hardware environments.
- **Clear written and verbal communication** of highly technical concepts to non-specialist audiences.
