The Operations & Business Development Manager is the commercial and logistical spine of NAC. This individual ensures that the technical capabilities of the production and engineering pillars translate into reliable client experiences, efficient supply chains, and sustained revenue growth. The role spans inbound client acquisition and quoting, procurement and supplier management, cross-border logistics coordination, project intake and scheduling, and active pursuit of strategic partnerships and acquisition targets across the Hamilton–Fort Erie–Buffalo manufacturing corridor. As a founding team member, this person must be equally comfortable building a procurement process from a blank spreadsheet and walking the floor of a potential acquisition target.

## Core Responsibilities

- Own the client intake pipeline from initial inquiry through project scoping, quotation, statement of work, and order confirmation; maintain CRM records and pipeline metrics.
- Develop and manage NAC's supplier relationships including PCB fabricators, component distributors, stencil manufacturers, and consumables vendors; negotiate pricing, lead times, and preferred supplier agreements.
- Coordinate cross-border logistics for components, assemblies, and tooling transiting between Ontario and New York State; maintain compliance with CUSMA/USMCA trade documentation requirements and broker relationships.
- Build and manage the master production schedule in coordination with the Production Lead, balancing client delivery commitments against equipment capacity and labour availability.
- Identify, research, and pursue acquisition targets — PCB fabrication shops, CNC/precision machine shops, and sheet metal operations — with particular attention to succession-planning owners in the Niagara–Hamilton corridor.
- Support grant and incentive applications including NRC IRAP submissions, SR&ED documentation, Canada Small Business Financing Program applications, and municipal incentive programs such as the Fort Erie Gateway CIP.
- Track and report on key operational and commercial metrics including on-time delivery, quote-to-order conversion, gross margin by service line, and supplier performance.
- Manage vendor contracts, NDAs, supply agreements, and client purchase orders in coordination with legal counsel as required.
- Represent NAC in municipal, regional, and binational economic development forums; build relationships with Hamilton Economic Development, Invest Niagara, Buffalo Niagara Partnership, and related bodies.

## Required Qualifications

- Minimum 5 years of experience in operations management, supply chain, or business development within a manufacturing or technology services environment.
- Demonstrated experience managing procurement and supplier relationships for physical goods, ideally in an electronics or precision manufacturing context.
- Familiarity with Canadian cross-border trade documentation (CUSMA/USMCA), customs brokerage processes, and tariff classification basics.
- Experience with project scheduling, capacity planning, and production coordination in a multi-client environment.
- Proficiency with ERP, CRM, and project management software; advanced proficiency in spreadsheet-based financial modelling.
- Strong negotiation and relationship management skills with demonstrated success in supplier or partner negotiations.

## Preferred Qualifications

- Experience identifying and evaluating acquisition targets or executing mergers and acquisitions in an SME context.
- Familiarity with Canadian government funding programs, particularly NRC IRAP, SR&ED, and provincial/municipal incentive structures.
- Cross-border business development experience in the Ontario–New York corridor.
- Background in electronics contract manufacturing, precision machining, or related industrial sectors.
- P.Log., SCMP, or equivalent supply chain designation.

## Competencies

- **Commercial instinct:** the ability to identify and close growth opportunities without sacrificing operational quality.
- **Systems builder:** capable of designing and implementing operational processes in the absence of inherited infrastructure.
- **Cross-functional integration:** translating client requirements and strategic priorities into actionable schedules and supply chain decisions.
- **Regional fluency:** deep familiarity with or rapid ability to develop relationships in the Hamilton–Niagara–Buffalo business community.
