## The Reindustrialization Mandate

Supply chain disruptions, geopolitical risk, and federal reshoring incentives have created unprecedented demand for domestic electronics production capacity. Programs such as Canada's National Research Council IRAP grants, the Canada Small Business Financing Program, and Ontario's regional development initiatives, combined with New York State's START-UP NY program and NYPA's low-cost hydropower access, create a rare alignment of policy, capital, and geography favoring exactly what NAC is building.

## A Binational Advantage

The Niagara Peninsula is uniquely positioned as a binational production corridor. Fort Erie and Buffalo are separated by the Peace Bridge — one of the busiest commercial crossings in North America. NAC will establish legal and operational presences on both sides of the border from the outset, enabling clients to access continental supply chains, SUNY research partnerships, and US federal procurement opportunities while benefiting from Ontario's manufacturing incentive environment.

## Why Now. Why Here.

The conditions for NAC's success are converging in a way that will not persist indefinitely. Reshoring incentives are at a historic high, but they are time-limited. The cohort of aging owner-operators in the Niagara manufacturing base is at peak succession pressure — a window for acquisition at reasonable valuations that will close as other acquirers recognize it. And the academic and talent infrastructure in both Hamilton and Buffalo has reached a level of sophistication that makes workforce development for precision electronics both achievable and scalable.
