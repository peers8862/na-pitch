<div class="timeline">

  <div class="timeline-item active">
    <div class="timeline-dot">1</div>
    <div class="timeline-phase">Phase 1 — Now</div>
    <div class="timeline-title">Hamilton Anchor</div>
    <p class="timeline-desc">NAC's inaugural facility in Hamilton, Ontario will serve as the operational and strategic nerve center of the network. Hamilton's industrial heritage, available commercial real estate, post-secondary institutions, and transit infrastructure make it the natural anchor for a precision electronics operation.</p>
    <ul>
      <li>4,000–8,000 sq. ft. facility configured for end-to-end small-batch assembly, test, and packaging</li>
      <li>IPC-A-610 compliant assembly environment with ESD controls, cleanroom-adjacent workflow zones</li>
      <li>In-house firmware development lab for embedded systems optimization</li>
      <li>Training and demonstration space for workforce and partner education programs</li>
      <li>Administrative and network coordination hub for the broader NAC ecosystem</li>
    </ul>
  </div>

  <div class="timeline-item future">
    <div class="timeline-dot">2</div>
    <div class="timeline-phase">Phase 2 — Near-Term</div>
    <div class="timeline-title">Fort Erie Foothold</div>
    <p class="timeline-desc">Fort Erie represents NAC's most strategically important early expansion. Proximity to the Peace Bridge crossing provides direct operational continuity with the Buffalo-Niagara market. NAC will establish office and hosting facilities in Fort Erie to serve both Ontario and New York State actors — a physical presence that signals binational commitment and facilitates the legal and logistical structures required for cross-border production flows.</p>
  </div>

  <div class="timeline-item future">
    <div class="timeline-dot">3</div>
    <div class="timeline-phase">Phase 3 — Expansion</div>
    <div class="timeline-title">Buffalo-Niagara Registration</div>
    <p class="timeline-desc">NAC will register as a US entity in New York State from the outset of operations, with first-phase relationships centered on SUNY Buffalo's electrical engineering and computer science programs on the North Campus. These academic partnerships enable access to research capabilities, co-op talent pipelines, and START-UP NY benefits including tax abatements and subsidized operational costs for partnering companies.</p>
    <p class="timeline-desc">NYPA's low-cost hydropower rates available to eligible manufacturers in Western New York represent a meaningful operational cost advantage for energy-intensive assembly and test operations — a differentiated input cost that strengthens NAC's competitive positioning against offshore alternatives.</p>
  </div>
