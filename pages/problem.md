The North American electronics production landscape is fragmented: assembly houses, firmware consultancies, and technical training providers operate as independent silos. Clients requiring end-to-end support — from prototype to tested firmware-optimized unit to certified packaged product — must manage five or more separate vendor relationships, accept inconsistent quality standards, and absorb significant coordination overhead.

Our research confirms no competitor currently integrates all three pillars — assembly, firmware depth, and education — into a single coordinated offering. This is NAC's core market position.
